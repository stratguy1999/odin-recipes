Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a pasta-based dish consisting of multiple layers of flat noodles with Passata, mozzarella and beef in the middle. It originated in Italy many years ago. It is cooked in the oven using a deep, ovenproof dish.

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 750g beef mince
  • 90g pack prosciutto
  • 800g Passata
  • 200ml beef stock
  • nutmeg
  • 300g lasgna sheets
  • white sauce
  • 125g ball mozzarella

Steps

  1. Heat 2tbsp olive oil in pan and cook beef mince for about 10 minutes until browned all over.
  2. Finely chop 4 slices of prosciutto, then stir through the meat mixture.
  3. Pour over 800g passata and 200ml beef stock. Add a little grated nutmeg, then season.
  4. Bring up to a boil, then simmer for 30 mins until the sauce looks rich.
  5. Heat the oven to 180C and lightly oil an ovenproof dish (about 30 x 20cm).
  6. Spoon one third of the meat sauce into the dish, then cover with some fresh lasagna sheets from a 300g pack. Drizzle over roughly 130g of white sauce.
  7. Repeat until you have three layers of pasta. Cover with the remaining 390g white sauce, making sure you can't see any pasta poking through.
  8. Scatter 125g torn mozzarela over the top
  9. Arrange the rest of the prosciutto on top. Bake for 45 minutes until the top is bubbling and lightly browned.
